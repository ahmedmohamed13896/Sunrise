About Us

Darrin Rogers, Clinical Director

MSW, LCADC, CCS

Darrin Rogers is the Clinical Director for Sunrise Detox Center in tirling, NJ. Darrin is a native of New Jersey, growing up in nearby Paterson and has been in the addiction field for 18 years. He began his work with Sunrise Detox in 2010 as a part time counselor. After joining the company on a full time basis in 2011, he became the Clinical Director in 2012, where he enjoys teaching others how to become social workers and counselors as well as providing the best possible detox care.


Darrin attended Eastside High School where he was a band section leader and played the Trombone. His high school is also where the movie “Lean on Me” was filmed. After high school, Darrin completed his undergraduate work at Kean University in Union, receiving his bachelor’s degree in Social Work. He went on to earn his Master’s degree in Social Work from Fordham University. When not at work, Darrin is involved with his local church, jogs three to four times a week, and practices Tae Kwon Do.

Dr. Francis Rienzo, Medical Director 

Dr. Rienzo has been in private practice in internal medicine more than 20 years and has been involved in the field of addiction medicine since the early 90's. He is the co-founder and Medical Director of Jersey Shore Daytox, Medical Director of Discovery Institute for Addictive Disorders, and Clinic physician for Jersey Shore Addiction Services. He is also employed by NJ Sports and Exposition authority as an in-house physician.

Dr. Rienzo is an active treatment advocate for Reckitt Benckiser pharmaceuticals and is often called upon to train physicians in the proper treatment with Suboxone. He is a diplomate of American Board of Internal Medicine and a diplomate of the American Board of Addiction Medicine. And, is certified by the American Society of Addiction Medicine (ASAM).

Jean Bronock-Zaccone, Director of Nursing 

Jean has more than 30 years of experience in Behavioral Health, from staff nursing through the executive level. She began working in the field of addictions while in Europe and employed by the US Army. She has also served as a Vice President of nursing within a four hospital system, ensuring that all regulatory requirements were met including those for the Joint Commission.

She is an RN with a Masters degree in Administration from Fairleigh Dickinson University, and has served on a variety of Boards including Greystone Park State Hospital, Sussex VNA, and currently on New Views, a co-occurring treatment provider. She has been honored for her advocacy and leadership by the Boy Scouts of America, the United States Air Force, and the Garden State Women's Magazine.
