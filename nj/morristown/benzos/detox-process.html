Unsupervised Benzo Detox Can Be Terrorizing

Medical Detox Ensures Your Safety and Comfort.  Here's the Process:


The First Step

Recovery starts when you admit you have a problem and pick up the phone to get help. This is the most important part of the entire process. But it's also critical that you seek proper treatment because detoxing from Benzos without medical supervision can result in panic attacks, anxiety and seizures.

The longer you take benzos, the more your body will adapt to them, your anxiety levels will rise, and the more you'll need just to feel normal.  So don't put off getting the help you need.

When you call, a trained addiction advisor will answer your questions, check your insurance, and explain how medical detox alleviates withdrawal symptoms. You'll also discuss the process so you know exactly what to expect when you get here.

We can even pick you up from anywhere in the Stirling Metro area -- free of charge.

Upon Arrival

You'll be greeted by a caring intake specialist when you arrive at our clean, comfortable facility.  You'll get help checking in and getting settled in your room.  A nurse will check your vitals to start your medical detoxification program and make sure you're comfortable right from the start.

During Treatment

A team of caring, qualified doctors and nurses will monitor your physical detoxification to ensure a safe, comfortable withdrawal from Benzos. Counselors are here to address your emotional needs during this vulnerable time. Group and individual counseling is available but there's also plenty of free time to relax and watch TV in your room or in the common area. Delicious and healthy meals are prepared daily by the chef on staff.

The irony of Benzos is that they're prescribed for anxiety and loss of sleep.

But the longer you take them, the more they cause that.
